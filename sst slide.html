Why did people travel to India in ancient times from various parts of the world?

There are many reasons why people travelled to India:-

TRADE AND COMMERCE:-

India was well known for wealth and resources like spices, textile and some precious stones. Traders from Greece, Rome, Russia and China came to India to engage in commerce and create new trade routes.

RELIGIOUS PILGRIMAGES:-

India was a significant center for Buddhism, Hinduism and later, Islam. pilgrims from China, southeast Asia and middle east to India to visit sacred sites, study sacred texts, and seek spiritual enlightenment.

EDUCATION AND KNOWLEGDE:-

India was home to many renowned centers of learning. Scholars and students from various parts of the world came to study mathematics, astronomy, medicine and philosophy.

CULTURAL EXCHANGE:-

Travelers were motivated by the desire to experience and document the rich cultural heritage of India. They were fascinated by India's diverse culture, customs, language, and traditions which were recorded in their travelogues.

These motivations highlight India's historical significance as a hub of economic, spiritual and intellectual activity.

Which routes were used to reach India's favorable destinations?

The following were routes used in ancient times to travel to india from various parts of the world:-

-The silk route

-The salt route

-The amber road

-The spice route

-The Tin route

-The insence route

-The grand trunk route:- One of the oldest routes.

-The post track
